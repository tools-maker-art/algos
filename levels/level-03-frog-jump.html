Level 03

🐸 Frog & The Stones

Frog Jump

World A Basics

🎮 Objective

Help the frog reach the last stone with the minimum total jump-energy.

🧠 Problem (in tiny words)

Heights[i] is the height of stone i. From stone i the frog can jump to i+1 or i+2. Energy = |height diff|. Reach stone n-1 with minimum total energy.

🎭 Story

A frog is on stone 1. The pond has N stones each with a height. Each jump costs effort equal to the height difference. Saving lily-pads is life — minimise total tiredness.

😵 Brute force (the painful way)

Try both jumps from every stone recursively. Same subproblems show up everywhere.

🌱 From brute → recursion

cost(i) only needs cost(i-1) and cost(i-2). Pure 1D DP.

🔁 Recursion

cost(i) = min( cost(i-1)+|h[i]-h[i-1]|, cost(i-2)+|h[i]-h[i-2]| ).

# pseudo
cost(i) = min( cost(i-1)+|h[i]-h[i-1]|, cost(i-2)+|h[i]-h[i-2]| ).

🧩 Memoization

Cache cost(i).

🧱 Tabulation

dp[0]=0; dp[1]=|h[1]-h[0]|; for i≥2 use the recurrence.

🚀 Space optimisation

Two variables: prev2, prev1.

📊 Complexity

O(n) time, O(1) space.

💡 Key takeaway

Two-choice transitions = small lookback DP.

🎯 Mini challenge

What if the frog must visit stone 0 AND stone n-1 with min total energy? (Same recurrence.)

🎮 Game UI idea
