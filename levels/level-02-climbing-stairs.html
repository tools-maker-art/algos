🪜 Stair Sprint

Climbing Stairs

World A Basics

🎮 Objective

Count the number of unique ways to reach the top of a staircase taking 1 or 2 steps at a time.

🧠 Problem (in tiny words)

You’re at step 0. The top is step N. From any step you may jump 1 or 2 stairs. How many distinct paths exist?

🎭 Story

Imagine a tiny cat hopping up stairs. Sometimes it leaps two, sometimes one. How many different hop-stories can it tell after reaching the top?

😵 Brute force (the painful way)

Try every choice: at each stair branch into ‘+1’ and ‘+2’. Lots of repeated subproblems.

🌱 From brute → recursion

Ways(n) only depends on Ways(n-1) and Ways(n-2). Sound familiar? Yep — this is Fibonacci wearing a hat.

🔁 Recursion

ways(n) = ways(n-1) + ways(n-2). Base: ways(0)=1, ways(1)=1.

# pseudo
ways(n) = ways(n-1) + ways(n-2). Base: ways(0)=1, ways(1)=1.

🧩 Memoization

Cache ways(n) once computed.

🧱 Tabulation

dp[0]=1, dp[1]=1, dp[i]=dp[i-1]+dp[i-2].

🚀 Space optimisation

Two variables prev2, prev1.

📊 Complexity

O(n) time, O(1) space.

💡 Key takeaway

Many problems with two choices reduce to ways(n-1)+ways(n-2)-style recurrences.

🎯 Mini challenge

Modify it so the cat can hop 1, 2, or 3 stairs. (Hint: add ways(n-3).)

🎮 Game UI idea
