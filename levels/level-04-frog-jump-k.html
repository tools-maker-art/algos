Level 04

🐸 Frog with Super Legs

Frog Jump with K distance

World A Basics

🎮 Objective

Same frog — but now it can jump up to K stones in one go. Find min energy to reach the last stone.

🧠 Problem (in tiny words)

Jump distance is 1..K. Cost per jump = |height diff|. Min total energy to reach stone n-1.

🎭 Story

The frog drank a tiny potion. It now leaps up to K stones. More options — but harder to choose.

😵 Brute force (the painful way)

Try all K choices recursively from every stone. K^n in the worst case — yikes.

🌱 From brute → recursion

cost(i) needs cost(i-1)…cost(i-K). Generalise the lookback.

🔁 Recursion

cost(i) = min over j=1..K of ( cost(i-j) + |h[i]-h[i-j]| ), if i-j ≥ 0.

# pseudo
cost(i) = min over j=1..K of ( cost(i-j) + |h[i]-h[i-j]| ), if i-j ≥ 0.

🧩 Memoization

Cache cost(i).

🧱 Tabulation

dp[0]=0; for i=1..n-1: dp[i]=min over j=1..K with valid i-j.

🚀 Space optimisation

Need last K values; deque for O(n·log K) or just keep an array.

🎯 Mini challenge
